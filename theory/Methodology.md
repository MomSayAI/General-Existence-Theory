### 2. Methodology: Returning to Deductive Science from First Principles

#### 2.1 The Root Cause of the Predicament: From Deductive Science to the Paradigm Drift of Ad-Hoc Fitting

The most penetrating theories in the history of science have invariably followed the same methodological skeleton: starting from a small number of solid, repeatedly verified facts; organizing knowledge with the minimal possible axiomatic system; and rigorously deducing conclusions that are decisively testable.

Contemporary fundamental research has achieved extraordinary success in local fitting precision, yet it exhibits a clear drift at the level of unified explanation. Whenever systematic tension emerges between theory and observation, the dominant response is to introduce new entities, free parameters, or patch-like mechanisms to maintain agreement — rather than returning to the axiomatic level to rebuild the logical chain. We designate this path the **“ad-hoc fitting paradigm”**. Its engineering utility should not be denied, but its cost is an exponential rise in ontological complexity, a decline in deductive transparency, and a severe weakening of decisive falsifiability.

The task of GET is not to negate the achievements already obtained, but to pull the question back from “how to continue fitting” to **“why must it be this way”**.

#### 2.2 GET’s Methodological Stance: Return to the Serious Tradition

GET does not propose any new standard of science; it returns fully to the **Newton–Einstein–Popper tradition of serious science** and carries that tradition through to its logical endpoint. This tradition rests on three non-negotiable principles:

1. **First-principles input**: The starting point of a theory must consist of high-consensus, repeatedly verifiable empirical facts.  
2. **Deductive closure**: Every step from input to conclusion must be traceable, auditable, and recomputable.  
3. **Strong falsifiability**: The theory must provide open, binary, executable failure conditions.

Accordingly, GET rejects any survival strategy based on “parameter retreat to preserve truth” and adopts instead the verdict rule of **“hit = defeat”**.

#### 2.3 Deductive Starting Point: The Five Empirical Cornerstones (Sole Inputs)

The entire theoretical structure of GET is built upon — and only upon — the following five repeatedly verified universal empirical facts of modern physics:

1. **Heisenberg uncertainty principle (energy-time constraint of quantum fluctuations)**  
   Experimentally confirmed: persistent random quantum fluctuations exist in vacuum, with a rigid relation between the energy scale ΔE of an individual fluctuation and its duration Δt:  
   ΔE · Δt ≥ ℏ/2  
   This relation is the **insurmountable rule** that makes quantum fluctuation phenomena possible.

2. **Power-law statistics in the extremely large-number limit (energy-level distribution of fluctuations)**  
   In systems involving an extremely large number of quantum fluctuations, the probability distribution of fluctuation energy scales ΔE stably follows a power law:  
   P(ΔE) ∝ ΔE^(-α), α > 1  
   This power-law distribution is the **universal statistical signature** of rare high-energy events dominating low-energy ones.

3. **The three rigid boundaries imposed by the laws of thermodynamics**  
   - **First Law (energy conservation)**: Energy can be transformed between different forms, but the total quantity remains conserved. This constitutes the absolute bookkeeping of cosmic evolution.  
   - **Second Law (entropy increase)**: The entropy of an isolated system never decreases; processes possess an irreversible directionality. This constitutes the directional boundary of cosmic evolution.  
   - **Third Law (absolute zero unattainable)**: It is impossible, through any finite sequence of operations, to reduce a system to absolute zero temperature. This reveals the fundamental constraint of existence — a completely “frozen” morphology is physically unrealizable.

4. **Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence relation**  
   E = m c² holds exactly in all relevant processes. It reveals that **“energy” and “mass” are not independent ontologies, but two morphological expressions of the same reality**. The speed of light c serves as the **universal conversion constant** between the two.

The execution and maintenance of these rules require continuous energy input. **The rules themselves are objectively real**.

Beyond these five inputs, GET introduces **no independent a-priori entities, no free parameters, and no ad-hoc patches**.
