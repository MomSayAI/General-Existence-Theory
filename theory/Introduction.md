### 1. Introduction: From Fragmented Vocabularies to a Unified Grammar of Existence

Modern science has achieved high-precision descriptions at vastly different scales, yet it has long lacked a single, cross-scale generative grammar capable of unifying them. Quantum theory, cosmology, life sciences, and social sciences each possess formidable local strength, but at the level of “why is it possible for things to be this way,” they remain structurally fragmented. Whenever systematic tension arises between theory and observation, the prevailing strategy is to introduce additional entities, tunable parameters, or ad-hoc mechanisms to preserve fit. We term this approach the “ad-hoc fitting paradigm” and distinguish it sharply from the Newton–Einstein–Popper tradition of serious science: the latter begins from first principles, proceeds by deductive closure, and demands strong falsifiability as the ultimate arbiter.

Generalized Existence Theory (GET) is developed strictly within this serious tradition. GET introduces **no new a-priori entities**. Its sole inputs are the five empirical cornerstones already enumerated:

1. Heisenberg uncertainty (energy-time constraint of quantum fluctuations)  
2. Power-law statistics in the large-number limit  
3. The three rigid laws of thermodynamics  
4. Einstein’s mass-energy equivalence  
5. The rule-dependence of all structured, stable existence

The unified existence grammar is therefore:

Ω^(M) = Φ(L)

Existence is judged by the existence-degree equation (which determines sustainability); evolution is driven exclusively by the unified upgrading mechanism: **complexity grows only via geometric screening + the four-phase cycle (trial–capture–solidification–generation)**. Cosmic genesis, category differentiation, and hierarchical evolution are thereby no longer parallel patch narratives but different temporal stages of one and the same causal chain.
