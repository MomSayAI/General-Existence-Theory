**Axiom III (Morphology Existence Criterion, Evolutionary Direction, and Scale Constraints)**

**1. Morphology Existence Criterion**

Per Definition A1, the **instantaneous existence degree** Ψ_M(t) of any concrete morphology M instantiated by protocol set L is:  
Ψ_M(t) := E_solid(M,t)/τ_solid(L) + E_active(M,t)/τ_active(L) + η(L) · e(t)

**Survival criterion** — necessary and sufficient condition for continued existence of M:  
E_active(M,t)/τ_active(L) + η(L) · e(t) ≥ 0

**Three-state distinction**:
- **> 0**: healthy operational state — morphology M is sustained normally  
- **= 0**: critical equilibrium state — morphology M is fragile but not yet disintegrated  
- **< 0**: death state — morphology M disintegrates immediately

**Key explanations**:
- The term E_solid/τ_solid is passive structural yield from possessing solidified assets (e.g., gravitational binding in stars, skeletal support in organisms) — it provides long-term stability buffer but **does not participate in immediate survival**.  
- Immediate survival depends entirely on the last two terms.  
- When η · e(t) < 0, the system consumes E_active reserves to offset negative net flux. As long as E_active suffices, survival criterion remains satisfied and the system lives.  
- Persistent η · e(t) < 0 depletes E_active. When E_active falls to where E_active/τ_active + η · e(t) < 0, immediate operation cannot be sustained → instant death.  
- Solidified-state energy E_solid **cannot help** during immediate crisis because its conversion rate to active state is too slow.  
- After death, E_solid may be utilized by other systems, but the dead morphology itself cannot use it to save itself.

**Third Law embodiment**: Complete freeze-out (E_active = 0 and η · e(t) = 0) is unattainable — every existent morphology must maintain E_active/τ_active + η · e(t) ≥ 0, so ideal absolute-zero crystals are physically impossible.

**2. Dual Paths of Evolutionary Direction**

To maintain or increase existence degree Ψ_M(t), a system has only two proactively optimizable paths, directly arising from the structure of the existence-degree equation:

Ψ_M(t) = [E_solid(M,t)/τ_solid(L)] (passive structural yield — non-optimizable)  
+ [E_active(M,t)/τ_active(L) + η(L) · e(t)] (operational energy balance — decisive for survival)

**Important note**: The first term E_solid/τ_solid is passive yield inherent to the core protocol fingerprint C_𝒮 (e.g., gravitational binding in stars, skeletal stability in organisms, brand value in firms). It **cannot be actively optimized**; the system must accept it as given.

**Path 1: Increase active-state energy reserves**  
Goal: enlarge E_active/τ_active.  
Strategy: expand the scale of active-state energy E_active, providing longer operational buffer against flux fluctuations.  
Physical meaning: E_active is the system’s “working capital” (stellar nuclear fuel, organismal ATP, firm cash flow, civilization strategic reserves). Increasing E_active means greater resilience during negative flux episodes — but E_active is consumable and must be continually replenished via Path 2.

**Path 2: Maintain persistently positive net flux**  
Goal: ensure η(L) · e(t) > 0 at all times.  
Strategies:  
1. Raise capture-conversion efficiency η(L), optimizing protocol set L to more effectively transform environmental energy into usable form.  
2. Enlarge instantaneous net flux e(t) by opening new energy-input channels or reducing output losses, keeping net inflow positive.

Physical meaning: this is the **fundamental guarantee of immediate survival**. If η · e(t) ≤ 0, the system begins depleting E_active reserves. Persistent η · e(t) < 0 exhausts E_active → E_active/τ_active + η · e(t) < 0 → death.

**Death mechanism**  
- Negative-flux phase: η · e(t) < 0 → consume E_active reserves. Survival criterion still holds as long as E_active suffices.  
- Critical point: E_active depletes to where E_active/τ_active + η · e(t) = 0 → critical equilibrium.  
- Death point: further depletion → E_active/τ_active + η · e(t) < 0 → immediate inability to sustain operation → death.  
- Post-death: E_solid may be scavenged by others (microbial decomposition, asset acquisition in bankruptcy), but the dead system itself **cannot use E_solid** to self-rescue due to slow conversion rate.

**Stellar case illustration**:
- Molecular-cloud collapse (protostar): gravitational potential → heat + structure → η · e(t) > 0, net inflow, accumulating both E_active (core fuel) and E_solid (binding energy, rotation, magnetic fields).  
- Ignition: fusion triggers, radiation pressure expels cloud → η · e(t) jumps to negative.  
- Main sequence: η · e(t) < 0 persistent — star consumes mass (E_active) into radiation every second with no external input. Steady state is **mechanical equilibrium** (radiation vs. gravity pressure), **not energy equilibrium**.  
- Fuel depletion: E_active decreases, but consumption rate still supports criterion ≥ 0 → balance holds.  
- Critical: E_active falls to criterion = 0.  
- Death: further fall → criterion < 0 → hydrostatic equilibrium collapses → death.  
- Post-death: gravitational energy release (E_solid conversion) powers supernova or compact remnant formation — **corpse decomposition**, **not self-rescue**. Star cannot use its huge E_solid to restart fusion because conversion rate is too slow.

**Biological case illustration**:
- Normal: feeding maintains η · e(t) > 0, ATP/glucose sufficient.  
- Starvation: η · e(t) < 0 → first consume glycogen (part of E_active), then fat/muscle (E_solid). Fat → ATP conversion is slow.  
- Critical: E_active depletes and E_solid/τ_solid conversion cannot offset deficit → criterion < 0 → death. Body (E_solid) still largely present — this is the true meaning of “starving to death”: **not total energy depletion, but exhaustion of immediately mobilizable active-state energy**.  
- Post-death: corpse (E_solid) decomposed by microbes, but organism cannot use its own skeleton to sustain life.

**Firm case illustration**:
- Normal operation: revenue > expense → η · e(t) > 0, cash flow (E_active) sufficient.  
- Loss: η · e(t) < 0 → consume cash reserves.  
- Critical: cash exhausted, no financing → criterion < 0 → bankruptcy.  
- Post-bankruptcy: assets (E_solid) acquired by others, but bankrupt firm cannot pay wages with its own factory equipment.
