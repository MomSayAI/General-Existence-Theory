**2. Sayable existence: morphology M**  
Morphology M is the stable, observable collection of phenomena presented by protocol set L during recursive execution.  
M := Phenomenon of L running  
M is the **external phenotype** of L’s execution.

**3. Endogenous state variables**  
To describe the dynamics of L running (i.e., of M), we introduce variables that are entirely endogenous to the process. **All variables are directly generated from each execution of the difference function D, with no external input or a-priori assumption required.**

**a. Difference function D (core operation, generative engine of all variables, constrained by protocol set L)**  
D: Σ → Σ  
is the difference-generation function. Each execution of D produces a state transition and a difference value:  
σ_{t+1} = D(σ_t), difference value = D(σ_t) − σ_t

**b. Time t (temporal unfolding of D)**  
t is the sequential count of D iterations, t ∈ ℕ₀, starting from initial state σ₀. Each execution of D advances t by 1, recording the ordered unfolding of differences. The complete life cycle of a fluctuation entity corresponds to a finite number of steps τ_i = m_i · t_P (t_P is the minimal iteration unit).

**c. Instantaneous difference e(t) (scalar)**  
The **instantaneous difference** at step t is defined as the state change at that step:  
e(t) = D(σ_t) − σ_t  
e(t) is a scalar that carries its own sign (positive or negative): positive indicates energy borrowing, negative indicates energy repayment. It is an endogenous variable directly generated by each execution of D.

**Physical correspondence**: e(t) directly corresponds to the **instantaneous energy borrowing/repayment flux** in conventional physics.

**d. Net borrowed balance E(t) (energy, scalar)**  
The **net borrowed balance** up to step t is the algebraic sum of all instantaneous differences from birth to the current step:  
E(t) = ∑_{k=1}^t e(k) = ∑_{k=1}^t (D(σ_k) − σ_k)

**This is the endogenous definition of energy in GET** — energy is the cumulative algebraic sum of instantaneous differences, representing the net energy borrowed by the entity from birth to time t.

**Life-cycle evolution**:
- At birth: E(0) = 0  
- Rising phase: borrowing > repayment, e(t) > 0 dominates, E(t) monotonically increases  
- Peak: reaches maximum E_peak  
- Declining phase: repayment > borrowing, e(t) < 0 dominates, E(t) monotonically decreases  
- Annihilation: E(τ) = 0 (all borrowed energy is fully repaid)

This enforces the strict constraint “lifetime borrowing equals lifetime repayment” — net borrowed balance starts at 0, rises to a peak, and returns to 0. Throughout the entire life cycle, E(t) ≥ 0 holds strictly.

**Peak borrowed energy**:  
E_peak = max_{t ∈ [0,τ]} E(t)

**Endogenous expression of Heisenberg constraint**:  
E_peak · τ ≥ ℏ/2

This relation is the **real process constraint** of D itself: generating and accumulating non-trivial difference necessarily involves a finite process (τ ≥ minimal iteration unit) and requires that the product of peak net borrowed balance and life-cycle duration exceed a minimal constant. ℏ/2 is the **minimal energy-time compound cost** that L₀ must pay to produce and sustain an identifiable “existence entity”.

**Derivation of solidified-state and active-state energy**:

The net borrowed balance E(t) manifests in morphology M in two qualitatively distinct forms:

- **Solidified-state energy E_solid(t)**: the portion of energy irreversibly locked via protocol solidification. This portion **cannot be directly used for immediate survival maintenance**; its release or dissipation is governed by a characteristic **solidified-state time constant** τ_solid(L).  
  **Cross-domain universality note**: The physical meaning of τ_solid(L) varies by domain — nuclear decay half-life or stellar lifetime in physics, metabolic turnover of fat or bone remodeling in biology, fixed-asset depreciation cycle in economics, long-term memory forgetting time constant in cognitive science. Despite mechanistic differences, its mathematical role in the existence-degree equation is identical: it quantifies the intrinsic rate at which solidified-state energy can be converted back into immediately usable resources.

- **Active-state energy E_active(t)**: the freely disposable portion of total locked structural assets, used to support real-time operation of the morphology. This portion **can be directly consumed to maintain immediate survival** and is governed by an **active-state characteristic time constant** τ_active(L). In biology this corresponds to ATP, blood glucose, glycogen, etc. — the immediate energy currency.

Relation:

**Instantaneous net borrowing flux**: e(t) itself.
