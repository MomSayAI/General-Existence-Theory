A theory’s value lies not merely in its explanatory depth for known phenomena, but in its courage and capacity to predict the unknown — and to stake its survival on those predictions. We classify the predictions into four hierarchical levels according to their nature and mode of verification:

- **Level 2: Foundational Physics Strong Predictions** — decisive, all-or-nothing verdicts on core contemporary physics puzzles (dark matter, dark energy, essence of gravity). This is the **core trial** of the physical picture.

All GET predictions — especially those at Level 2 — are **binary judgments** with no room for statistical thresholds or parameter tuning. The theory either stands or falls. This is the thorough implementation of Popperian falsifiability and a return to Einstein’s ideal of “clear, decisive tests” in serious science.
